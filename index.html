The Real Picture

Most websites are failing disabled users — silently, every day.

96.3% of websites have detectable WCAG failures. That is not a niche concern — it is a growing legal liability that most businesses have no idea they are carrying.

Where most sites fail

  • Low contrast text — invisible to users with low vision or colour blindness
  • Missing alt text — images are completely silent for screen reader users
  • Keyboard traps — users who cannot use a mouse get stuck and cannot proceed
  • Unlabelled forms — inputs with no labels leave users confused and unable to complete purchases
  • ADA lawsuits rising — over 4,600 web accessibility cases filed in 2023 alone

What Level Up does

  • Full WCAG 2.2 audit — automated and manual testing across all 78 success criteria
  • We fix the code — our developers remediate issues directly, not just hand you a PDF report
  • Legal documentation — VPATs, accessibility statements, and audit trails your lawyers can use
  • Team training — equip your developers and designers so issues never come back
  • Ongoing monitoring — we stay with you so regressions are caught before they become lawsuits

WCAG 1.3.1 & 3.3.2 (A)
Warning
White text on yellow sale badge — 1.1:1 contrast
Minimum required is 4.5:1. The badge is effectively invisible to anyone with low vision.
WCAG 1.4.3 Contrast (AA)
Warning
Modal dialog does not trap keyboard focus
Tab key exits the modal into the background page — keyboard users cannot close or interact with the dialog.
WCAG 2.1.1 Keyboard (A)
Critical
Data tables built with div elements — no headers
Screen reader users hear raw numbers with zero context. No <th> or scope attributes anywhere in the dashboard.

Critical

Screen reader announces "button button button" with no indication of purpose. No aria-label or visually hidden text.
